Complete the online application. You will be asked a series of questions about you and your household as well as your income and expenses.

Submit the completed application. Your application will be sent to the nearest DHR office in your county.

The county office will update the application status periodically. An interview will be scheduled with a worker by phone or in person before you start to receive benefits.

Before you continue, please read the following

You have the right to file an application with only your name, address, and signature. If you choose to do so, please note we will be unable to automatically screen your application to determine if you qualify for expedited assistance as your application will not contain income or resource information.

You may get expedited assistance within 7 days if: Your household’s monthly gross income is less than $150 and your liquid resources (cash, checking, or savings) are less than $100 OR your household’s monthly rent/mortgage and utilities are more than your household’s income and liquid resources OR your household is a destitute migrant or seasonal farmworker household and your liquid resources are less than $100.

If you are eligible, your benefits will be determined from the day we receive your signed application. This means your first month of benefits will be prorated from the day we receive your application. If you submit your application using this website after 5:00 pm CST or on a weekend or holiday, it will be received the next business day. When a resident of an institution is jointly applying for SSI and food assistance prior to leaving the institution, the filing date on the application is the date of release of the applicant from the institution. You must be interviewed after your application is received. You will be notified of your appointment time and date. We must approve or deny your application within 30 days. Applications must be processed in accordance with SNAP/Food Assistance procedures, including timeliness, notice, and FH requirements regardless of whether the application is for SNAP and other programs. Also, a household may not be denied Food Assistance benefits solely because it has been denied benefits from other programs.

Information Needed To Process An Application

Please bring as much of the following information as possible with you when you come for your interview. This will help us in processing your application faster. With your cooperation, the Food Assistance Office will help you obtain the necessary proof to process your application if you need further assistance.

  1. Proof of your identity (examples: birth certificate, driver's license, school ID)
  2. Names and dates of birth of all persons who live in your home.
  3. Social Security Numbers for each household member. If an individual does not have a Social Security Number the Food Assistance Office will tell you how to apply for one. You only have to give social security numbers (SSN) and citizenship/immigration information for those household members that you are asking for food assistance benefits. You will have to give information such as income for household members who are not seeking benefits to determine if the persons for whom you are applying are eligible to receive benefits.
  4. Current check stubs, pay slips, or employer's statements that show the gross amount of money paid to all working persons in the home.
  5. A signed and dated statement from any person who gives money to you or to anyone in the household. The statement should show how much money is given, when and how often.
  6. Award letters or stubs that show the amount of unemployment compensation (pennies) paid to you or any other person in your household.
  7. Letters from Social Security, Railroad Retirement, Veteran's Administration, retirement association, or any other agency from which you or anyone in your household receives a check.
  8. Current proof of rent or mortgage payments, including lot rent, property taxes, and fire insurance on the home.
  9. Current receipts for utilities (gas, lights, water, telephone, garbage service).
  10. A signed and dated statement from any roomer and/or boarder showing the amount they pay and how often.
  11. Immigration and Naturalization Service (INS) identification forms if any member of your household is an alien.
  12. Current bank statements of savings and checking accounts in banks, savings and loan associations, credit unions, etc.
  13. Letter or other statement showing the value of any stocks or bonds owned by any household member.
  14. Signed and dated statements from the person providing care for a child or other dependent, including amount charged.
  15. If you have a household member who is 60 or older or a member who is disabled, your household may be eligible for a medical deduction. You will need to bring proof of their medical expenses, including health insurance policies, premiums, and reimbursements from any medical insurance policy, medicaid or medicare.
  16. Verification of any LEGALLY OBLIGATED child support paid by any member of your household to or for a non-household member. This includes the Legal Obligation (i.e. court order) and the amount they actually paid.

Important Information

Privacy Statement

The collection of this information is authorized under the Food and Nutrition Act, as amended. The information will be used to determine whether your household is eligible or continues to be eligible to participate in the program. We will verify this information through computer matching programs. We will also use this information to monitor compliance with program regulations and for program management.

This information may be disclosed to other Federal and State agencies, including the Income Eligibility Verification System(IEVS), for official examination, and to law enforcement officials for the purpose of apprehending persons fleeing to avoid the law.

Information provided will be verified using IEVS when discrepancies are found by the State agency and that such information may affect household eligibility and level of benefits.

Alien status of applicant household may be subject to verification by INS through the submission of information from the application to INS and the submitted information received from INS may affect the household’s eligibility and level of benefits.

If a claim arises against your household, the information on this application may be referred to Federal and State agencies, as well as private claims collection agencies, for claims collection action.

Providing the requested information, including SSN of each member, is voluntary. However, failure to provide an SSN will result in the denial of benefits to each individual failing to provide an SSN. Any SSNs provided will be used and disclosed in the same manner as SSNs of eligible household members.

NVRA

The National Voter Registration Act (NVRA) requires the Department of Human Resources to provide you with an opportunity to register to vote.

Applying to register or declining to register to vote will not affect the amount of assistance that you will be provided by this agency.

If you are already registered to vote at your current address, you do not need to re-register. If you would like to register to vote or would like to change your address for voting purposes, you may do so through the Secretary of State’s office at the following link: Secretary of State

If you would like help in filling out the voter registration form, we will help you. You may seek assistance with the application form at the time of your interview or by calling your local Department of Human Resources office located within your county. The decision to seek or accept help is yours. You may fill out the application form in private.

The Department of Human Resources will not use any information you submit related to voter registration for any other purpose.

If you believe that someone has interfered with your right to register or to decline to register to vote, your right to privacy in deciding whether to register or in applying to register to vote, or your right to choose your own political party or other political preference, you may file a complaint with the Secretary of State Capitol:
